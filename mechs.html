Mechanical Keyboards

Before entering the world of custom keyboards, one should first understand the differences between mechanical keyboards and membrane keyboards. The main difference between mechanical and membrane keyboards is the type of switch being used to trigger key presses. The image below shows the differences between a mechanical and membrane keyboard.

Mechanical Keyboard Switches

Pressing a mechanical keyboard switch moves a plastic panel out of the way so that two electrical contacts can meet. The switch then registers the key press, and the spring at the bottom alows the switch to return to its original position quickly.

Membrane Keyboard Switches

Membrane keyboard switches actuate by the user physically pushing a small contact at the bottom of the switch onto a receiver mounted along the floor of the keyboard when pressing the key cap.
