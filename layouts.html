Keyboard Layouts

In this section, we will talk about the stark differences between ANSI and ISO layouts on mechanical keyboards.

ANSI vs ISO: Explained

The acronyms, ANSI and ISO, stand for two different world's standards organisations. ANSI stands for the American National Standards Institute, and ISO stands for the International Organization for Standardization. These are both keyboard layouts that describe the size and position of the keys on a keyboard. While logical layouts such as QWERTY, Colemak and Dvorak determines the placement of alphabetical keys. ANSI and ISO keyboards differ in the size and orientation of the Enter key, Backslash and Left Shift keys.

The alt graph key is available on ISO keyboards and allows the user to access the third symbol on a key. This is common in other languages other than English. If there is a fourth symbol on the key, this is accessed by pressing Shift and Alt Gr.

The one-key difference between thje ANSI and ISO layouts exists in the left shift key. In ANSI keyboards, the left shift key is one large wide rectangular key. In ISO layouts, the shift key is broken into 2 diferent keys, making up that one-key difference.

In fully programmable keyboards, the extra button is what you make of it. It just allows you to add in another key. Perhaps in a different language, it could be a symbol or something else.
